### 1. GARY BECKER — Nobel 1992 — Human Capital Theory
**Prize citation:** "For having extended the domain of microeconomic analysis to a wide range of human behavior and interaction, including non-market behavior."

**What Becker proved:** Human beings invest in themselves and generate compounding returns. Education, training, and experience are capital — not consumption.

**What Becker left open:** He never built the measurement instrument. Human capital in Becker's model is a *stock* — a static accumulation. He assumed it was measurable without specifying how.

**ARCHON Ψ closes the gap:** QTAC₇ is the instrument Becker assumed but never formalized. CMPD (Compounding Velocity) is the dynamic flow measure Becker's model required but could not operationalize.

> *"Becker proved the asset class exists. ARCHON Ψ built the Bloomberg Terminal for it."*

---

### 2. JAMES HECKMAN — Nobel 2000 — Non-Cognitive Skills & Dynamic Skill Formation
**Prize citation:** "For his development of theory and methods for analyzing selective samples."

**What Heckman proved:** Non-cognitive skills — persistence, self-regulation, motivation — are as economically predictive as cognitive skills (IQ). The Heckman Curve shows investment in early skill formation compounds at 13%+ annual social return.

**What Heckman left open:** He measured non-cognitive skills in children via behavioral observation and longitudinal tracking. He never built a real-time, adult-stage, market-grade scoring instrument.

**ARCHON Ψ closes the gap:** ERR (Error Intelligence), ALGN (Alignment), and SOV (Sovereignty) are the adult-stage operationalization of Heckman's non-cognitive skill set. The Canon Ledger is the longitudinal tracking instrument Heckman's research demanded.

> *"Heckman proved non-cognitive skills compound. ARCHON Ψ scores them in real time at any age."*

---

### 3. AMARTYA SEN — Nobel 1998 — Capabilities Approach
**Prize citation:** "For his contributions to welfare economics."

**What Sen proved:** Economic welfare is not just income. It is the set of *capabilities* — what a person is able to do and be — that constitutes real freedom and real value.

**What Sen left open:** The Capabilities Approach is philosophical and qualitative. Sen never built a scalar scoring system that could rank capabilities for capital allocation purposes.

**ARCHON Ψ closes the gap:** QTAC₇ is the capabilities approach made quantitative. The Stone Score is what Sen's framework would look like if it had a number. The seven operators map directly to the seven core capabilities Sen identified: practical reason, affiliation, emotion, senses/imagination, play, control over environment, and bodily integrity.

> *"Sen proved capabilities are the real unit of value. ARCHON Ψ put a score on them."*

### 4. ANGUS DEATON — Nobel 2015 — Measurement of Human Welfare
**Prize citation:** "For his analysis of consumption, poverty, and welfare."

**What Deaton proved:** How you measure welfare determines what you see. Bad measurement produces invisibility of real human conditions. Good measurement is a moral act.

**What Deaton left open:** He measured welfare at the population level — consumption, poverty rates. He never descend to the individual cognitive architecture level.

**ARCHON Ψ closes the gap:** The Canon Ledger is Deaton's measurement rigor applied to the individual. The SBOM (Software Bill of Materials) per Canon block is the audit trail Deaton's methodology requires. The retrial date architecture (March 27, 2027) is pre-registered measurement — the gold standard Deaton advocated.

> *"Deaton proved measurement is moral. ARCHON Ψ makes individual cognitive output auditable."*

### 5. DANIEL KAHNEMAN — Nobel 2002 — Behavioral Economics / Cognitive Bias
**Prize citation:** "For having integrated insights from psychological research into economic science, especially concerning human judgment and decision-making under uncertainty."

**What Kahneman proved:** Human cognition is systematically biased. System 1 (fast, intuitive) and System 2 (slow, deliberate) operate differently under pressure. Most high-stakes decisions are made by System 1 pretending to be System 2.

**What Kahneman left open:** He described the bias architecture. He never built a scoring framework that could distinguish System 2 performance from System 1 performance at the individual level for investment purposes.

**ARCHON Ψ closes the gap:** CRIS (Crisis Architecture) specifically measures System 2 performance under pressure — the moment when System 1 hijacks System 2. The ERR operator measures whether the subject learns from System 1 errors or repeats them. High CRIS + high ERR = demonstrated System 2 dominance under load.

> *"Kahneman proved the bias exists. ARCHON Ψ scores whether the subject has overcome it."*

---

### 6. RICHARD THALER — Nobel 2017 — Nudge Theory / Behavioral Insights
**Prize citation:** "For his contributions to behavioural economics."

**What Thaler proved:** Small structural changes in choice architecture produce large behavioral changes. The environment shapes the decision, not just the agent.

**What Thaler left open:** Nudge theory is population-level and environmental. It does not score the individual's resistance to nudge — their cognitive sovereignty.

**ARCHON Ψ closes the gap:** SOV (Sovereignty) is the anti-nudge operator. It measures whether the subject defines quality by their own Canon or by the dominant regime's defaults. High SOV = structurally resistant to Thaler's nudge architecture. This is the operator that identifies true cognitive independence vs. crowd-following intelligence.

> *"Thaler proved the environment captures most agents. ARCHON Ψ scores who escapes it."*
