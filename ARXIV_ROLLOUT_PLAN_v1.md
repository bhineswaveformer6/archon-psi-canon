## THE RELATED WORK SECTION — OUTLINE

Five areas to position against:

**1. Human Capital Theory (Becker 1964, Mincer 1974)**
- Becker established that human beings invest in themselves and generate returns
- Gap: no measurement unit. QTAC₇ provides the unit Becker assumed but never formalized.

**2. Psychometric and Personality Assessment (Big Five, MBTI, Hogan)**
- Existing assessments are snapshot-based, self-reported, and not calibrated for longitudinal compounding
- Gap: no trajectory, no verification, no capital market application. QTAC₇ is longitudinal and outcome-calibrated.

**3. Algorithmic Management and Workforce Analytics (Numerics, Visier, Workday)**
- Current workforce analytics measure activity, not cognitive quality
- Gap: measure outputs of labor (hours, tasks) not quality of cognition. QTAC₇ measures the cognitive substrate.

**4. Distributed Ledger Attestation (Verifiable Credentials, W3C DID)**
- Blockchain attestation systems provide tamper-evidence but not scoring
- Gap: attestation without scoring is an unsigned check. QTAC₇ provides the score; the VOLT ledger provides the attestation.

**5. AI Evaluation Frameworks (HELM, BIG-Bench, MMLU)**
- These benchmark AI cognitive performance but not human-AI collaborative cognitive capital
- Gap: as AI handles execution, the relevant measurement target shifts from task performance to cognitive architecture quality. QTAC₇ addresses this shift.

## LIMITATIONS SECTION — DRAFT

What QTAC₇ does NOT measure:
1. **Domain expertise depth** — a world-class surgeon and a world-class investor may score identically on QTAC₇ because it measures cognitive architecture, not domain knowledge
2. **Short-term state fluctuations** — the Stone Score is a trait measure. A subject in acute crisis will have the same Stone Score as in peak state. (This is addressed by the H-CAP state overlay in the Trinity pipeline, but that is a companion methodology, not part of QTAC₇ itself)
3. **Moral quality** — SOV measures self-determination, not ethical goodness. A high-SOV subject may be sovereignly committed to harmful objectives.
4. **Cultural portability** — the framework was developed and calibrated against a North American/Western European operator context. Cross-cultural validation is required before deployment in other contexts.
5. **Small sample calibration** — the predictive validity claims require a cohort of 50+ scored founder objects tracked through retrial dates. We have 0 retrials completed as of April 2026. The March 27, 2027 retrial date for the first public calibration event is the first test.
